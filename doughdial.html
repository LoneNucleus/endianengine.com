Decarboxylate THC flower

This page is only for decarboxylating dry cannabis flower by itself. It is not a butter, oil, olive-oil, or infusion recipe. Follow local laws, label finished material clearly, and keep it away from children, pets, and unsuspecting adults.

Tools

Oven thermometer Glass Pyrex baking dish Ground cannabis flower Spoon or spatula Jar for cooled flower

Method

Preheat oven to 240°F.
Use an accurate oven thermometer as needed. A home oven is not a scientific instrument, and the dial may not match the real temperature. Many home ovens run hot, so use a proper oven thermometer when possible.
Prepare the flower.
Grind the cannabis flower and spread it flat in a glass Pyrex baking dish. Keep it in an even layer.
Start the first 10 minutes.
Once the thermometer confirms the oven is actually at 240°F, place the dish in the oven for 10 minutes.
Stir #1.
Remove the dish, stir the flower thoroughly inside the dish, spread it flat again, then return it to the oven for another 10 minutes.
Stir #2.
At 20 minutes total oven time, remove, stir thoroughly, flatten again, and return for another 10 minutes.
Stir #3.
At 30 minutes total oven time, remove, stir thoroughly, flatten again, and return for the final 10 minutes.
Finish at 40 minutes total.
Remove the dish and let the flower cool fully. Once cool, transfer it to a clearly labeled glass jar, preferably a mason jar with a lid.

Important temperature note

Keep the target at 240°F. Higher oven temperatures can degrade cannabinoids and terpenes. If your oven is drifting toward 250°F or higher, lower the oven setting and wait for the thermometer to settle before continuing.

Use note

Once cooled, the decarboxylated flower is ready to use in edibles. You do not have to make butter first. Butter, oil, olive oil, and other infusions are separate recipe choices, not required for this basic decarb step. Decarboxylated flower can be added directly to suitable recipes when measured, mixed evenly, and labeled clearly.

What it is

A dumb little cover screen for Android.

MyStupidApp started as a bored weekend experiment. First there was a walking stick figure. Then other dumb things happened. Eventually the stick figure lost the argument and the app became an animated scene: spinning windmill, flying birds, moon, barn, sunset, and motion that gives your screen something harmless to show.

Open it

Launch the APK on Android when you want a full-screen visual cover instead of whatever was previously visible.

Let it sit

It stays up as a simple animated scene. Touching it is not the point. It is there to be looked at, ignored, or used as a casual screen cover.

Swipe away

When you are done, close it like any other Android app. Swipe up or use your normal app switcher behavior.

Actual use cases, somehow

It does almost nothing, which is why it is easy to understand.

Casual screen hider

Open it when you want your phone to show a harmless animated scene instead of the app, message, browser, or chaos underneath.

Desk toy

Leave it running while the phone sits nearby. It is more interesting than a black rectangle and less useful than a spreadsheet.

Freebie app

No sales lane. No deep platform strategy. Just a free Android thing that exists because not every app has to justify its own annual report.
